B.Sc In Mathematics


General Information


1. Course Structure:


The B.Sc. in Mathematics program is a three-year undergraduate course designed to provide a strong foundation in mathematical concepts and their applications.


2. Specializations:


While Maharaja College may not offer specific specializations within the B.Sc. in Mathematics program, the curriculum can be tailored to focus on areas like:

  • Pure Mathematics:

    • Number theory, algebra, geometry, analysis

  • Applied Mathematics:

    • Statistics, operations research, mathematical modeling

  • Computational Mathematics:

    • Numerical analysis, scientific computing


3. Eligibility Criteria:


  • 10+2 with a minimum percentage (varies by specializations) with CBSE or any other equivalent board of examinations
  • Age Criteria (usually 17-25 years)


4. Admission Process:


  • Online Or Offline Application
  • Entrance exam (if required)
  • Document verification
  • Counselling


Course Structure And Syllabus


1. Core Subjects:


Calculus, Algebra, Geometry, Trigonometry, Number Theory, Mathematical Analysis, Numerical Methods, Statistics.


2. Specialized Subjects:


Depending on the focus area, students might study topics like:

  • Pure Mathematics:

    Abstract Algebra, Real Analysis, Topology

  • Applied Mathematics:

    Operations Research, Mathematical Modeling, Statistics

  • Computational Mathematics:

    Numerical Analysis, Scientific Computing, Computer Programming


3. Project Work:


Students might be required to undertake a research project or a mathematical modeling project.
